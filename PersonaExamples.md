1. Technology-Challenged Customer Needing Basic Training
Customer admits lack of proficiency with the app and requests help learning basic features. They may be in the field and need immediate guidance on downloading storm maps or using mobile features. 1 Team should offer flexible training options including immediate tech support, scheduled Zoom sessions, and patience with technology concerns.
2. Confused About Coverage Area and Pricing
Customer is uncertain about which states/cities are included in their current plan and needs clarification before renewal. 2 They may believe they have coverage they don’t actually have, or want to understand pricing for adding additional territories.
3. Budget-Constrained Customer Seeking Flexibility
Customer expresses strong satisfaction with the product but faces current budget constraints that prevent immediate renewal. 3 They may request postponed payment, split payments, or reduced features to make the service affordable.
4. Leadership Change or New Decision-Maker
New stakeholder joins organization mid-contract and is unfamiliar with existing tools and their capabilities. 4 Previous leadership may have purchased features that were never properly leveraged or adopted by the team.
5. Customer Working Outside Coverage Area
Customer discovers they are working in a location that falls outside their Hail Trace coverage area, preventing them from accessing storm data for active jobs. 5 They may not have realized territorial limitations when they went to inspect damage.
6. Account Access Issues Due to Email Problems
Customer’s primary email was hacked or changed, preventing receipt of reports and renewal notifications. 6 Multiple users may need to be set up, or account ownership needs to be transferred to a new secure email.
7. Confusion Between Pause and Cancellation
Customer believes they cancelled but actually paused subscription, leading to unexpected charges when service automatically resumed. 7 Previous agent may have promised to process cancellation but never followed up.
8. Unpaid Invoice Blocking Access Before Major Weather Event
Customer has unpaid invoice blocking system access right before an active weather weekend when they need the service most. 8 Decision maker may be unavailable or asking questions about feature utilization before approving payment.
9. Perceived Poor Value Due to Limited Storm Activity
Customer paid for annual plan but received no usable storm leads because all storms were outside their service radius, resulting in perceived waste of money. 9 They question whether to renew when storm activity in their area is inconsistent year-over-year.
10. Underutilizing Features Due to Lack of Training
Customer purchased service months ago but never completed onboarding or training, missing key functionality. 10 They may be unaware of valuable integrations with their existing CRM or other features included in their plan.
11. Duplicate Services Creating Cost Concerns
Customer already uses another service that provides similar data and questions whether they need to keep paying for both. 11 They need clarity on what unique value each service provides and whether features overlap.
12. Notification Overload from Wrong Settings
Customer receives excessive notifications for storms in areas outside their coverage region, causing frustration. 12 USA-wide alerts may be enabled when they only need local notifications, or they’re receiving daily forecasts for irrelevant states.
13. Expecting Refund After Limited Usage
Customer used service during winter months when weather prevented them from working, or they had technical difficulties that prevented full utilization. 13 They may feel entitled to refund or extension due to seasonal business constraints or system issues.
14. Competitive Threat from Lower-Priced Alternative
Customer is evaluating or has already switched to a competitor offering lower pricing. 14 They may cite specific competitor prices and features, requiring value differentiation and possible price matching to retain them.
15. Payment Method Issues Causing Service Interruption
Customer’s card on file has expired, been replaced due to fraud, or hit credit limit, causing payment failures and account suspension. 15 They may need immediate reactivation while traveling to a storm location or during critical business period.
