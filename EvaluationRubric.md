# Evaluation Rubric v1

**Focus:** Discovery Quality, Sandler Reversing, Questioning Strategy, Customer Pain Identification  
**Use case:** Training simulations for CS / retention reps

---

## Scoring Scale

Use a **1-5 scale** for each category.

**5 — Excellent**  
Consistently strong, natural, effective

**4 — Good**  
Mostly strong, minor gaps

**3 — Adequate**  
Acceptable but uneven or incomplete

**2 — Weak**  
Noticeable issues that limited effectiveness

**1 — Poor**  
Missed the category almost entirely

---

## Core Categories

### 1. Opening and Problem Framing
**What this measures:**  
How well the rep starts the conversation, sets the tone, and opens space for discovery.

**Strong behaviors**
- Opens calmly and professionally
- Invites the customer to explain the issue in their own words
- Avoids jumping into solutions too early
- Signals curiosity rather than defensiveness

**Weak behaviors**
- Starts pitching too quickly
- Interrupts or redirects too early
- Assumes the issue before confirming it
- Sounds transactional or rushed

**Scoring guidance**
- **5:** Opens clearly, earns engagement, and creates room for real discussion
- **3:** Opens adequately but moves too quickly toward solving
- **1:** Opens poorly and immediately narrows the conversation or becomes reactive

---

### 2. Discovery Question Quality
**What this measures:**  
Whether the rep asks useful questions that uncover the real situation.

**Strong behaviors**
- Asks open-ended questions first
- Moves from broad to specific
- Seeks context before recommending action
- Uses follow-up questions that build on customer responses
- Clarifies facts instead of guessing

**Weak behaviors**
- Asks mostly closed yes/no questions
- Uses generic or repetitive questions
- Fails to follow up on important clues
- Asks questions that feel scripted and disconnected
- Misses key context about timeline, usage, decision-making, or desired outcome

**Scoring guidance**
- **5:** Questions are thoughtful, layered, and move the conversation forward
- **3:** Some useful discovery, but gaps remain
- **1:** Very little meaningful discovery

---

### 3. Sandler Reversing and Questioning
**What this measures:**  
How well the rep avoids over-explaining and instead uses the customer’s statements to deepen the conversation.

**Strong behaviors**
- Uses the customer’s words to prompt expansion
- Responds with questions instead of immediate defenses
- Asks the customer to clarify vague statements
- Uses reversal naturally, without sounding evasive
- Helps the customer talk through their own reasoning

**Examples of good reversing**
- “When you say it hasn’t been worth it, what are you comparing that to?”
- “Can you say more about what made it feel frustrating?”
- “What were you hoping would happen that didn’t happen?”
- “When you say pricing is the issue, is it the total amount, the timing, or the return?”

**Weak behaviors**
- Defends too early
- Explains features before understanding the concern
- Treats objections as statements to counter rather than topics to unpack
- Uses artificial or robotic reversal phrases
- Misses opportunities to explore emotionally loaded comments

**Scoring guidance**
- **5:** Reversing is natural, controlled, and consistently productive
- **3:** Some good use of reversal, but not sustained
- **1:** Rep mostly reacts, explains, or argues instead of exploring

---

### 4. Identification of Customer Pain
**What this measures:**  
Whether the rep identifies the real pain point behind the presenting issue.

**Strong behaviors**
- Distinguishes surface complaint from root pain
- Identifies operational, financial, emotional, or credibility pain
- Recognizes urgency and business consequences
- Confirms pain rather than assuming it
- Gets the customer to describe impact in their own words

**Types of pain to listen for**
- **Financial pain:** cost, cash flow, wasted spend, unpaid invoice pressure
- **Operational pain:** can’t access needed data, poor setup, underuse, coverage mismatch
- **Emotional pain:** frustration, distrust, feeling misled, overwhelm
- **Business risk pain:** missing storms, losing jobs, delayed decisions, poor ROI

**Weak behaviors**
- Stays at the symptom level
- Misses the impact of the problem
- Fails to identify what matters most to the customer
- Confuses annoyance with true business pain
- Doesn’t test whether the issue is urgency, trust, value, or budget

**Scoring guidance**
- **5:** Clearly identifies root pain and its impact
- **3:** Identifies some pain but not fully
- **1:** Misses the real pain entirely

---

### 5. Follow-Up Depth
**What this measures:**  
How well the rep keeps digging after the first answer.

**Strong behaviors**
- Uses second- and third-level questions
- Explores consequences, timing, ownership, and stakes
- Stays with important threads instead of bouncing topics
- Knows when a vague answer needs clarification

**Weak behaviors**
- Stops after first answer
- Changes topic too quickly
- Accepts vague statements at face value
- Fails to explore impact or motivation

**Scoring guidance**
- **5:** Consistently gets below the surface
- **3:** Some follow-up, but not enough depth
- **1:** Minimal follow-up

---

### 6. Listening and Use of Customer Information
**What this measures:**  
Whether the rep actually uses what the customer says to guide the conversation.

**Strong behaviors**
- References the customer’s prior statements accurately
- Adjusts questions based on what was learned
- Notices contradictions or ambiguity and explores them
- Summarizes key pain points clearly

**Weak behaviors**
- Ignores or forgets important details
- Repeats questions already answered
- Follows a script instead of the conversation
- Misses signals the customer is giving

**Scoring guidance**
- **5:** Actively listens and builds intelligently from customer input
- **3:** Listening is adequate but inconsistent
- **1:** Rep appears scripted or inattentive

---

### 7. Avoiding Premature Solutions
**What this measures:**  
Whether the rep earns the right to recommend something.

**Strong behaviors**
- Delays solutions until the problem is clear
- Avoids discounting before understanding the issue
- Does not jump to training, billing, coverage, or retention tactics too early
- Shows discipline in diagnosis

**Weak behaviors**
- Offers solutions too soon
- Uses discounts or feature explanations as a reflex
- Treats the first stated problem as the whole problem
- Pushes toward resolution without enough evidence

**Scoring guidance**
- **5:** Strong control and patience before prescribing
- **3:** Some premature solving, but discovery still happened
- **1:** Mostly solutioning with weak diagnosis

---

## Overall Rating Buckets

### Excellent Session
- Rep uncovers real pain
- Uses strong open-ended discovery
- Reverses naturally instead of defending
- Builds a clear picture before solving
- Makes customer feel heard and understood

### Adequate Session
- Rep gets some useful information
- Shows some discovery skill
- Identifies part of the pain
- May rush or miss depth, but conversation still progresses

### Weak Session
- Rep asks shallow questions
- Misses root pain
- Explains too early
- Reacts instead of reversing
- Leaves major gaps in understanding

---

## Automatic Strength Signals
Use these as positive markers:
- Rep asks “What’s driving that?” or equivalent
- Rep separates stated issue from actual impact
- Rep clarifies vague statements like “it’s too expensive” or “it wasn’t worth it”
- Rep identifies whether the issue is value, timing, trust, training, access, or budget
- Rep summarizes pain before recommending next steps

## Automatic Deductions
Use these as negative markers:
- Rep pitches before discovery
- Rep defends pricing immediately
- Rep ignores emotional language from the customer
- Rep asks mostly yes/no questions
- Rep misses obvious follow-up opportunities
- Rep assumes the problem without confirming it

---

## Suggested Weighted Score

- Opening and Problem Framing — **10%**
- Discovery Question Quality — **25%**
- Sandler Reversing and Questioning — **25%**
- Identification of Customer Pain — **25%**
- Follow-Up Depth — **10%**
- Listening and Use of Customer Information — **5%**

**Total = 100%**

---

## Evaluator Output Format

After each training session, output the following:

### Score Summary
- Opening and Problem Framing: X/5
- Discovery Question Quality: X/5
- Sandler Reversing and Questioning: X/5
- Identification of Customer Pain: X/5
- Follow-Up Depth: X/5
- Listening and Use of Customer Information: X/5
- Overall Score: X/5

### What the rep did well
- 2 to 4 bullets

### What the rep missed
- 2 to 4 bullets

### Stronger questions the rep could have asked
- 3 to 5 examples

### Pain points identified
- List the pain points the rep surfaced
- List the pain points the rep missed

### Coaching recommendation
- One short paragraph on what to improve next

---

## Simple Pass / Fail Version

**Pass**
- Asked meaningful open-ended questions
- Used reversal at least a few times
- Identified the customer’s main pain
- Did not rush to solution too early

**Fail**
- Stayed shallow
- Defended or pitched too early
- Missed root pain
- Did not use questioning to deepen the conversation

---

## Recommended v1 Standard

For a rep to be considered successful in v1, they should:
- Score at least **3/5** in every core category
- Score at least **4/5** in either Discovery Question Quality or Identification of Customer Pain
- Avoid major premature solutioning
