# Transcript Scoring Format v1

**Purpose:** Standard output template for scoring completed training transcripts  
**Use case:** Post-roleplay evaluation for CS / retention training scenarios

---

## Instructions for Evaluator

Use this format after a transcript or live roleplay has ended.

Guidelines:
- Score based on what the rep actually said, not what they may have intended
- Prioritize discovery quality, questioning depth, Sandler-style reversing, and identification of customer pain
- Be specific and concise
- Quote or paraphrase the rep’s behavior when useful
- Do not introduce new customer facts that were not present in the conversation
- Do not coach during the scoring sections until the coaching section at the end

## Evaluator Standards

### A strong score should reflect
- Clear, relevant open-ended discovery
- Useful follow-up questions
- Natural reversing instead of immediate explanation
- Accurate identification of customer pain
- Evidence that the rep listened and adapted
- Discipline before moving into solutions

### A weak score should reflect
- Shallow or scripted questioning
- Early pitching or defensiveness
- Missed root pain
- Poor follow-up depth
- Ignoring emotionally significant statements
- Generic or unsupported coaching feedback

---

## Scoring Reminders
- Do not over-score a friendly rep with weak discovery
- Do not under-score a direct rep if questioning was effective
- Separate politeness from actual skill
- Focus on what was surfaced, what was missed, and what changed the conversation
- Reward clarity, control, curiosity, and depth
